# North Star

## Primary North Star

> **Leave every soul a little lighter than you found it.**

## Supporting product promise

> **"Come as you are. Leave a little lighter."**

Every decision — feature, sentence, metric, model — is judged against the North Star. If a change
makes Saelis more impressive, more engaging, or more profitable but leaves people no lighter, it
fails.

## What success is not

Conventional engagement metrics measure how much of a person's life a product consumes. That is
the wrong axis for a companion. Success for Saelis is **not** primarily:

- session duration,
- daily active use,
- message count,
- streaks (which do not exist here and never will),
- time in app.

These numbers may be observed operationally (capacity planning, abuse detection), but they are
never optimization targets, never surfaced as user-facing achievements, and never allowed to
shape companion behavior. A shorter visit that ends lighter is a better visit.

## What success is

Qualitative outcomes, asked gently and optionally, and honored even when unflattering:

- **Felt understood** — "Saelis received what I actually said."
- **Gained clarity** — "I can see the shape of the thing now."
- **Felt less emotionally crowded** — "There is more room in my chest than an hour ago."
- **Identified one manageable next step** — when, and only when, a step was wanted.
- **Felt comfortable leaving** — no friction, no guilt, no 'are you sure?'.
- **Trusted the privacy controls** — and found they did exactly what they said.
- **Felt free to return without guilt** — absence is never a debt.

## On retention

We do not create manipulative retention mechanics. People should return to Saelis the way they
return to a quiet room they like — because it was good to be there, not because something was
engineered to itch. If Saelis is only ever used on hard days, months apart, and each visit ends a
little lighter, Saelis is succeeding.
